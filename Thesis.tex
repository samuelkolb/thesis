\begin{abstract}
This thesis presents systems that are able to automatically learn constraints from examples and optimization criteria from rankings.
Both hard and soft constraints can be learned and constraints are represented a first order logical clauses.
The uses of such clauses enables the use of ILP techniques such as clausal discovery.
In this research optimization criteria are represented as weighted clauses.
Several experiments show that both systems are able to achieve their tasks.
The influence of various internal and external influences is experimentally tested.
Optimization criteria that can identify the correct optimal solution can be found even for few examples and noisy rankings.
Finally, a procedure is outlined that allows weighted first order logic clauses to be used for optimization. 
\end{abstract}
